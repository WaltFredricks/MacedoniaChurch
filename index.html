Our History

Macedonia Baptist Church, located at 803 Pearl Ave S, Douglas, GA 31533, stands as a cornerstone of the African American community in Coffee County, Georgia. As a historic Southern Baptist congregation, it embodies resilience, faith, and community service amid the challenges of the Jim Crow era and beyond.

Early 1920s

The earliest documented reference appears in a 1922 edition of The Douglas Enterprise, where Rev. I. M. Young is identified as its pastor, highlighting its role in the community during segregation.

1926

Mrs. W. S. Swearingen delivers a welcome address on behalf of the church at the Georgia Annual Conference, signaling its growing prominence within South Georgia's Baptist networks.

Today

Continuing as a vibrant congregation with the motto "No Limits," emphasizing boundless faith, community outreach, and active social media presence.
